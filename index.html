4.1. Noether’s Theorem

  • This theorem is the core in the utilizing symmetries in classical and non-classical field theory.
  • The Energy-Momentum Tensor 𝒯︀𝜇𝜈 is the conserved current for spacetime translation.

Starting with the translation

𝜙(𝑥)→𝜙(𝑥+𝜉)=𝜙(𝑥)+𝜕𝜈𝜉𝜈𝜙(𝑥)+…

This provides 𝛿𝜙𝛿𝜉𝜈=𝜕𝜈𝜙 which applies universally regardless of the field 𝜙 (and in fact regardless of the physical object, as it applies to the Lagrangian ℒ︀, a consequence of the fact that 𝛿𝑆=0).

We then have that

𝛿ℒ︀𝛿𝜉𝜈=𝜕𝜇(∑𝑛𝜕ℒ︀𝜕(𝜕𝜇𝜙𝑛)𝛿𝜙𝑛𝛿𝜉𝜈)=𝜕𝜇(∑𝑛𝜕ℒ︀𝜕(𝜕𝜇𝜙𝑛)𝜕𝜈𝜙𝑛)𝜕𝜈ℒ︀=𝜕𝜇(∑𝑛𝜕ℒ︀𝜕(𝜕𝜇𝜙𝑛)𝜕𝜈𝜙𝑛)

We then have that

𝜕𝜇(∑𝑛𝜕ℒ︀𝜕(𝜕𝜇𝜙𝑛)𝜕𝜈𝜙𝑛−𝑔𝜇𝜈ℒ︀)=0

Where we then obtain

𝒯︀𝜇𝜈≡(∑𝑛𝜕ℒ︀𝜕(𝜕𝜇𝜙𝑛)𝜕𝜈𝜙𝑛−𝑔𝜇𝜈ℒ︀)

and 𝜕𝜇𝒯︀𝜇𝜈=0.

  • Energy density:

    ℱ︀=⨁𝑛ℋ︀𝑛

  • For Old Fashioned Perturbation Theory we utilize perturbation by setting the Hamiltonian to

  • Recursion provides

    𝑇=𝑉+𝑉ΠLS𝑉+𝑉ΠLS𝑉ΠLS𝑉+⋯
